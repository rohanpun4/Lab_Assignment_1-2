Nepal

Kathmandu, Nepal's capital, is set in a valley surrounded by the Himalayan mountains. At the heart of the old city’s mazelike alleys is Durbar Square, which becomes frenetic during Indra Jatra, a religious festival featuring masked dances. Many of the city's historic sites were damaged or destroyed by a 2015 earthquake. Durbar Square's palace, Hanuman Dhoka, and Kasthamandap, a wooden Hindu temple, are being rebuilt.

About Nepal

  • Nepal is a landlocked country located in South Asia.
  • It is bordered by China to the north and India to the south, east, and west.
  • Home to Mount Everest, the highest mountain in the world.
  • Nepal has a diverse geography, including mountains, hills, and plains.
  • Kathmandu is the capital and largest city of Nepal.
  • Nepal is known for its rich cultural heritage, including Hindu and Buddhist traditions.
  • The country has a population of over 30 million people.
  • Nepali is the official language, but there are many ethnic languages spoken throughout the country.
  • Tourism is a major industry in Nepal, with visitors coming to trek in the Himalayas, explore ancient temples, and experience Nepalese culture.
  • Nepal celebrates various festivals throughout the year, including Dashain, Tihar, and Buddha Jayanti.
